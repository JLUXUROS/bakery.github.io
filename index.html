Kalihim Bread

Kalihim bread is one of the popular items in Filipino bakeries, and for good reason.
With perfectly soft buns encasing a vibrant filling made of day-old bread,
eggs, milk, butter, vanilla, and red food coloring, it's a stand-out in looks and taste!


Pandecoco

Pan de Coco is a a soft bread roll stuffed with sweetened grated coconut meat.
It's a popular Filipino snack sold in most local panaderias or bakeries.


Crinkles

Chocolate crinkle cookies have become a holiday staple, but they're also great any time of year.
They're cake-like cookies made with unsweetened cocoa powder, vegetable oil, and a handful of other pantry staple ingredients.


Kababayan

Kababayan, which literally translates to fellow countryman, is Pinoy sweet muffin known for its golden brown exterior and distinct domed top shape.
It got its name due to its resemblance to a salakot, a native straw hat Filipino farmers and townspeople wore when out and about.


Ensymada

This Filipino classic ensaymada recipe is so yummy and a very popular sweet pastry among Filipinos.
